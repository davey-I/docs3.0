Charging a 14500 Li-ion Battery (XQ ICR 600 mAh, 3.7 V)

This guide explains how to safely charge a 14500 Li-ion battery using a lab power supply. It covers voltage and current settings, charging phases, and how to estimate charge time.

Power Supply Setup

     
  • Set voltage: 4.20 V for full charge or 4.00 V to prolong battery lifespan
    •  
  • Set current limit: Around 0.3 A (300 mA), which is 0.5C for a 600 mAh cell
    •  
  • Connection: Power supply positive (+) goes to battery positive, negative (−) to battery negative
    •  
  • Do not reverse polarity — it can damage the cell or cause overheating

Charging Phases

     
  • Constant Current (CC): Current stays fixed, voltage rises as battery charges
    •  
  • Constant Voltage (CV): Once target voltage is reached (e.g. 4.0 V), voltage stays constant and current begins to drop
    •  
  • Charging is complete when current falls below about 0.05–0.06 A (50–60 mA)

Charging Example

     
  • Battery voltage at start: 3.25 V
    •  
  • Power supply set to: 4.0 V
    •  
  • Initial current: 0.201 A (in CC mode)
    •  
  • Later: Voltage reached 3.9 V, still 0.201 A
    •  
  • Then switched to CV mode: Voltage held at 4.0 V, current dropped to 0.156 A and falling

Estimate Charging Time

     
  • Formula: Charging time ≈ (Battery capacity / Charging current) × 1.2
    •  
  • Example: (600 mAh / 201 mA) × 1.2 ≈ 3.0–3.3 hours
    •  
  • Note: Last 10–20% of charge takes longer due to current tapering in CV mode

Best Practices

     
  • Use 4.0 V instead of 4.2 V if you want to extend battery lifespan (lower stress)
    •  
  • Monitor charging current — it should drop as the battery gets full
    •  
  • Disconnect manually when current drops below 60 mA, or use a timer as a fallback
    •  
  • Battery should stay cool; warm but not hot is normal during charging
