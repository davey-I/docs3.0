Lingo

General

Long: Being long on a stock, means making money as the chart is going up.
Short: Meake money as the stock is going down the chart. ( Figure out how ?? )
Bull: Think the market goes up, expect a Long situation
Bear: Think the market goes down, expect a Short situation

Bid, Ask and Spread: Refere to the prices which Buyers and Sellers are willing to trade their stock.
Bid ist the highest a buyer is willing to pay for its stock. Ask is the lowest that a seller is willing to sell its stock.
The delta between those two is called the Spread.

Order Types

Market
I want the stock right now, no matter the price. The broker should buy instantely
    + you are in the trade, no matter what
    - you are buying to whatever price is set

Limit
Enter if the stock goes down to a certain price.
    + Get stock at cheaper price
    - Price may never be reached, no order at all

Stop
Only enter, if stock goes up to a certain price.

Stoploss
If already entered the trade, we can set this to say: 'exit if price goes down to this'.

Functionality-Purpose

Why do I need a Broker ?

A broker acts as the bridge or connection between you and the market. Today these are of course Online
and in Software, but for ex. back in the days, a Broker was a person, you had to call to place a Order. So to
be able to access the market we need a Online Broker.


How to get an Online Broker ?

Basically we have to choose a Online Broker for ex Webull, and then answer some questions etc. and Open a Account
Same as if you would open up a Bankaccount.

After the account is opened, we can download the Software provided by the Broker. We also have to deposite some
Money onto our Account. This can be done with a Creditcard, but might be a bad Idea, because we should only
trade with Money that we actually own and that we don't need very urgent.


How to access money we made while trading ?

If we made trades and made a plus on our account, we can get that money same ways as we put money onto our Account
If there will be transaction fees or not, depends a bit on the Broker, but mostly, there should be no fees if we set up a direct
Transaction of some kind.
