IPV4-IPV6

Pitfalls-Only-Use-IPV6

IPV6 and IPV4 are two different Protocols. To be sure a client can reach a site or a service
we would have to configure a both protocols, there are still a lot of unsolved problems on the way through the web.
As of 2025 there are still only about 40-50% adoption of IPV6, dependng on the country you are in. Among these
may also face problems on their way through the Web.


What needs to be compatible ?


To successfully reach your IPv6-only web server, the entire path from the user's browser to your server must support IPv6:

  • The Client OS & Browser : All modern OSes (Windows, Linux, macOS, Android, iOS) and browsers support IPv6.✅
    But support doesn't mean it's working. It must be properly configured and Enabled in order to work.

  • The Client’s Network (ISP, Router, etc.): This is the biggest potential point of failure. A client must:  
       
    • Get a globally routable IPv6 address from their ISP (not just link-local).
      •  
    • Have their home router configured to support IPv6, including correct prefix delegation, DNS, firewall rules.
      •  
    • Have an ISP that routes IPv6 traffic correctly
      •  

  • Middleboxes and Firewalls: Corporate firewalls, VPNs, mobile carrier NATs, or content filters often block IPv6 to simplify
    monitoring or due to lack of support. Also Some Wi-Fi hotspots or hotels explicitly block IPv6 for similar reasons.
